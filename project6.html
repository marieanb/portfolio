A study on multitasking on smartphones

My roles in the project: experimental researcher.

This study compares split-screen multitasking on smartphones during travel planning with traditional app switching.
Results show split-screen use reduces task completion time without compromising accuracy. Users find the feature intuitive and beneficial, yet adoption varies,
suggesting interface customization is crucial.
This research highlights split-screen functionality potential to enhance mobile task performance, emphasizing user preference considerations for future studies.

Reflection This project taught me how to evaluate mobile interface features and analyze user efficiency and preferences. One challenge was collecting consistent feedback from participants about their split-screen experience. We addressed this by designing a clear, focused survey to capture their insights. Overall, the study showed me the importance of user-centered design in mobile multitasking.
