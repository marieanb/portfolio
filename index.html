Hi! I'm Maria Anbinderytė, and I’m a UX designer passionate about Interactive Art and Human Motion.

My background is quite diverse, I got a Bachelor's degree in Bioinformatics at Vilnius University, and have completed internships as a frontend and backend software developer. I'm currently in my second year of a Master’s program in Human-Computer Interaction at Utrecht University.

Alongside my technical experience, I’m also a professional dancer. I’ve collaborated with artists on various live performances and TV shows. This unique combination of skills drives my interest in exploring the intersection of human motion, interaction technology, and art direction. I’ve been fortunate to gain hands-on experience in the combination of all of these areas, particularly during my work in a Motion Capture and Virtual Reality lab at Utrecht University.

I believe great design goes beyond aesthetics--it's about understanding users’ needs, behaviors, and emotions. That’s why I enjoy engaging directly with users, so that I can empathize with their experiences and create truly user-centered solutions.

In the Projects section, you'll find examples of my work in user research, interaction design, and motion capture.
