Investigating the Impact of Scent on Visual Attention and Memory

My roles in the project: experimental researcher, data analyst.

The research focused on two key questions
RQ1 – Do participants exposed to a scent during a learning task recall more words related to that scent than words not related to the scent?
RQ2 – Do participants spend more time looking at words associated with the scent and do they look at them more frequently?

The project started with a pre-study to identify the appropriate scent (a citrus car air freshener), followed by a pilot study to refine the workflow (see fig. 3). Finally, the main study was conducted to collect data.
Participants were asked to memorize words related to the citrus scent from a word cloud
(see fig. 1 and fig. 2).

The Eye-tracking Technology (see fig. 1) was used to measure participants' focus of attention and duration of gaze on certain words, along with a Verbal Learning Test to track how many and which words were recalled.

Result
Participants exposed to lemon scent during a verbal learning task recall more words associated with this scent, as compared to participants not exposed to an olfactory stimulus, however, the impact of scent on memory might not be immediate.

Reflection This project taught me how to design and analyze experiments to understand how scent affects memory and attention. Main challenge was figuring out the eye-tracking software and data, as small errors could affect results; We spent extra hours in the lab to make sure the setup is right. The experience improved my skills in experimental research and data analysis.
